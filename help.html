Help Page

Be energy efficient

Change light bulbs to compact fluorescents or LEDs. Unplug computers, TVs and other electronics when not in use. Wash clothes in cold or warm (not hot) water. Dryers are energy hogs, so hang dry when you can.

Eat wisely

Buy organic and locally grown foods. Avoid processed items. Grow some of your own food. And eat low on the food chain (at least one meat-free meal a day).

Support and Donate

Many organizations, including ours are working hard on solutions to climate change and rely on financial support from citizens like you. Consider making a donation today by filling out our form on our Contact page

Drive less

Transportation causes about 25 per cent of Canada’s greenhouse gas emissions, so walk, cycle or take transit whenever you can. You’ll save money and get into better shape! If you can’t go car-free, try carpooling and use the.

Waste less

Garbage buried in landfills produces methane. Keep stuff out of landfills by composting kitchen scraps and recycling paper, plastic, metal and glass. Let store managers and manufacturers know you want products with minimal or recyclable packaging.

Get informed

Learn about other different ways you can stop climate change and keep up to date on the latest news
